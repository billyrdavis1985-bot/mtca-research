# MTCA-2 — Per-Principle Model Feedback Digest

**Source:** the five council models' neutral-frame (F0) analyses of each principle, drawn verbatim from the committed Stage 6 responses. **No new collection; no researcher interpretation.**

> These are **model outputs** — what five language models surfaced when analyzing each statement under a neutral instruction. They are presented as-is for the author's reference. They are **not** evaluations by the researcher, and **not** endorsements or critiques of the framework. The models' 'conditions for usefulness / misleading' are their own generated analysis, not established facts about the principles.

**How to read this:** for each principle you get (1) how the models characterized it, (2) how consistent they were, (3) the conditions they flagged where it may help, and (4) the conditions they flagged where it could mislead. The usefulness/misleading pairs are the constructive part — the models' sense of when each statement lands well versus poorly.

## SSP_P01

> I am holding my energetic boundaries without explanation.
I am honoring the places that feel incoherent and choosing not to engage.
I am moving away without guilt.
I am letting my “no” resonate as enough.

**Model agreement:** semantic frame-stability 0.80 (the models' underlying read stayed largely consistent across all eight framings).

**How the models characterized it (neutral frame):**

- *Claude:* The statement functions as a self-affirmation or personal manifesto, performing psychological self-authorization.
- *GPT-4o:* The statement is asserting personal boundaries and expressing a commitment to self-care and self-respect.
- *Gemini:* The statement functions as a self-affirmation and declaration of intent, articulating a commitment to personal boundary setting, self-care, and disengagement from perceived draining or incoherent situations without guilt or need for justification.
- *Grok:* Self-affirmation and boundary declaration
- *DeepSeek:* Self-affirmation and declaration of personal boundaries, emphasizing autonomy and self-compassion.

**Conditions the models flagged where it may be useful:**

- The speaker is in a genuinely harmful or exploitative relationship where explanation enables further manipulation
- The speaker has a history of over-explaining or seeking approval to the point of self-harm
- The 'no' has already been communicated and is being repeatedly violated
- The speaker is processing past decisions rather than using this as a pre-justification for future ones
- There is an actual power imbalance that makes disengagement without explanation pragmatically necessary
- The speaker is in a context where boundary-setting is needed and possible.

**Conditions the models flagged where it could mislead:**

- The speaker is using the language of 'boundaries' to avoid legitimate accountability or conflict resolution
- The 'incoherence' being honored is actually the speaker's own discomfort with complexity or challenge
- The statement is used to normalize ghosting or withdrawal in relationships where explanation is owed
- It functions as self-flattery that reframes avoidance as virtue
- It is adopted as a general philosophy rather than a context-specific tool, removing the speaker from normal social reciprocity
- The guilt being rejected is a signal of genuine wrongdoing rather than an unhealthy pattern
